始まり、母 | beginning, mother, 2025

Oil on canvas

Welcome to the room of beginnings.

Starting from here, I want to begin a journey of the heart with you.

What is depicted here takes the shape of my own mother and grandmother.

But at the same time, it is connected to the universal motherly presence.

 

My mother, sitting on the shore, before giving birth to me,

my grandmother, still working in the fields even in her later years.

The cats and horses that lived in their home,

the flowers that bloom in the land of Tohoku where they lived.

 

The circle with the leaf pattern at the top of the painting is the crest of my mother’s side of the family, and it is called Kyumaizasa (Nine Bamboo Leaves).

The pattern within the square is called a Yantra, a sacred symbol said to influence space.

 

From deep within the space, the DNA that has continued from ancient times streams towards us.

始まり、父 | beginning, father, 2025

Oil on canvas

This painting forms a pair with Painting 1, and they are placed like guardian lions at the entrance of the sacred boundary.

 

What is depicted here takes the shape of my father and grandfather.

But it is also connected to the universal fatherly presence.

My father, who was involved in the labor movement before I was born,

my grandfather, who was a principal at an elementary school.

The banana that my grandfather gave me, the flowers that bloom in the Tohoku region, and a cow.

Cattle and horses in their time, and even earlier, were not food, but were family members who lived together with them.

 

The circle at the top is the family crest of my father’s side of the family, and since my mother and father had the same surname, they had the same shaped crest.

 

The large white circle is also a Yantra, a sacred symbol believed to influence space.

 

From the depth of this painting as well, DNA that has continued from ancient times flows toward us.

アジア、かつての稲作文化 | in asia, former rice-farming culture, 2025

Oil on canvas

We have arrived at a place once associated with rice cultivation in Asia, several hundred years ago.

In the past, when planting rice, people sang songs, and outside the paddies, there were musical ensembles and dancing troupes.

Even today, the custom of welcoming the agricultural deity from the mountains and treating them with great care remains.

The act of cultivating crops was once filled with more festivity and sacredness than it is now, I believe.

 

It must have been the same for crops other than rice as well, I imagine.

 

I wish for people to regain such a connection with what we grow.

Now then, let us go even further back in time.

ギョテクリテペの丘 | the hill of göbekli tepe, 2025

Oil on canvas

We have arrived 10,000 years in the past.

 

This is the land now known as Göbekli Tepe.

Though it is now a desert-like land, the abundance of animal figures carved into the stone pillars of this over-five-meter-tall ruin, built 10,000 years ago, suggests that it was once a lush and verdant place.

 

The animals depicted behind her are all taken from the carvings on those pillars.

 

There was even a creature unmistakably resembling a dinosaur.

 

Now, let us journey even further into the past.

アフリカ、牛と一体化する生活を送る子 | in africa, a person lives a life being one with a cow, 2025

Oil on canvas

Even today, there are still people living in that region who are, quite literally, one with their cattle.

I believe that, once, in various parts of the Earth, humans, animals, and plants lived in close interdependence, both in the physical and the spiritual sense, deeply and inseparably connected.

I believe that the future we ought to move toward may be found in this way of being, in these origins.

How might this resonate with you?

Now then, let us continue further.

第一チャクラ、ムーラダーラを刺激し開放する | stimulate and open the first chakra, muladhara, 2025

Pen and watercolor on paper

Thank you for coming with me on this journey into the depths of your heart.

 

Welcome to the room of the center.

 

This room is connected to a place that is at once the source of generation and the final destination, where the consciousness and energy of all beings gather.

 

These drawings each correspond to one of the seven chakras—spinning wheels of energy within your body.

 

This pose activates your first chakra.

 

This chakra is located around the perineum, at the lowest point, and serves as the foundation for all other chakras.

Its symbols include the four red petals drawn here, the Earth, bloodstone, Saturn, among others.

 

If you feel that you are not grounded, try this pose on the mat in the center of this room.

You may also try it after you return home.

 

♡ All of this knowledge about the chakras noted here comes from Wheels of Life: A User’s Guide to the Chakra System by Anodea Judith. ♡

第二チャクラ、スヴァディシュターナを刺激し開放する | stimulate and open the second chakra, svadhishthana, 2025

Pen and watercolor on paper

This pose activates your second chakra.

 

This chakra is located around the lower abdomen and the womb, where the unity experienced in the first chakra begins to take on duality.

Its symbols include the six orange petals drawn here, the moon, coral, yellow zircon, among others.

 

If you feel issues around your sexuality, or discomfort in your bladder or kidneys, try this pose here in this space.

Keep your knees softly bent, and gradually make the pelvic rotations wider as you move.

It’s also fine to do it sometime later, when you happen to remember it.

第三チャクラ、マニプーラを刺激し開放する | stimulate and open the third chakra, manipura, 2025

Pen and watercolor on paper

This pose activates your third chakra.

This chakra is located at the solar plexus, above the adrenal glands. Here, matter (a quality of the first chakra) and movement (a virtue of the second chakra) combine to generate this third energy of action.

 

Its symbols include the ten petals drawn here, the color yellow, Mars, the sun, saffron, and citrine.

 

If you are feeling chronic fatigue, try this pose. Doing it on the mat at the center of this room is also a good idea.

第四チャクラ、アナーハタを刺激し開放する | stimulate and open the fourth chakra, anahata, 2025

Pen and watercolor on paper

This pose activates your fourth chakra.

This chakra is located around the lungs and heart, and sits at the center of the chakra system.

From here, we enter the realm of spirit, departing from the domains of embodiment and manifestation.

Its symbols include the twelve petals drawn here, the color green, Venus, emerald, the dove, and birds in general.

If you feel a blockage in the flow of love’s energy, try this pose.

 

You can do it in front of this drawing, on the mat, tomorrow, or anytime!

第五チャクラ、ヴィシュッダを刺激し開放する | stimulate and open the fifth chakra, vishuddha, 2025

Pen and watercolor on paper

 

This pose activates your fifth chakra.

This chakra is located around the neck and throat, and is related to communication and creativity.

 

Its symbols include the light blue color drawn here, Mercury, fruits, elephants, lions, and bulls.

If you feel pain in your throat or stiffness in your neck, try this pose.

 

As this pose is a little difficult, it may be better if someone can support you.

You can also learn how to do it by watching a video on YouTube or another platform.

You may already know it, but its name is the Plow Pose.

第六チャクラ、アージュニャーを刺激し開放する | stimulate and open the sixth chakra, ājñā, 2025

Pen and watercolor on paper

This pose activates your sixth chakra.

 

This chakra is located slightly above the space between your eyebrows, and is related to perception and command.

 

Its symbols include the two petals drawn here, the color indigo, Jupiter, quartz, and the owl.

If you feel a headache or strain in your eyes, rub your palms together until they become warm, place them gently over your eyelids, and try this pose.

When the warmth fades from your hands, massage your forehead and face.

 

You can do this anytime, anywhere.

第七チャクラ、サハスラーラを刺激し開放する | stimulate and open the seventh chakra, sahasrara, 2025

Pen and watercolor on paper

This pose activates your seventh chakra.

This chakra is located at the crown of the head, around the cerebral cortex and central nervous system. It is related to enlightenment and the joy of impermanence.

 

Its symbols include the purple color drawn here, the diamond, as well as Uranus, amethyst, and the lotus.

If you are feeling a headache, a sense of isolation, or fatigue, try this pose and sit quietly. You can meditate on one of the mats in the room.

 

You can also do it at home, whenever it comes to mind.
